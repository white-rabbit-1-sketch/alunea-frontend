Here, you'll find answers to common questions about mailboxes. If you have a question that's not listed below, feel free to reach out to us for assistance.

What is a mailbox?

A mailbox in Alunea is a representation of the user's actual email address. It serves as the destination for emails forwarded from aliases. Essentially, it is your primary email inbox within Alunea.

What happens if I delete a mailbox?

Once deleted, all emails forwarded from aliases to that mailbox will no longer be accessible. Additionally, all aliases associated with the mailbox will be removed. However, you can always recreate a mailbox after deletion. Keep in mind that aliases won't be automatically restored.

How does a mailbox work?

When you create aliases in Alunea, you can choose to forward emails sent to those aliases to your designated mailbox. Any emails sent to aliases associated with your mailbox will be automatically forwarded to your primary email inbox.

What happens if I enable/disable a mailbox?

Enabling a mailbox ensures that emails forwarded from aliases are delivered to your primary inbox. Disabling a mailbox temporarily stops the forwarding of emails to your mailbox. Emails sent to aliases associated with a disabled mailbox will not be forwarded until the mailbox is re-enabled.

Why verify email of mailbox?

Verifying email of mailbox ensures that the mailbox address belongs to you, enhancing security measures. Until your mailbox is verified, emails will not be forwarded to it.

Can I mark a mailbox as a favorite?

Certainly. You can do it on the settings page.

What does it mean to mark a mailbox as favorite?

Marking a mailbox as favorite ensures it's automatically chosen when adding a new alias, enhancing convenience.
